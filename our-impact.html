We Raised €138,000!
Get Involved for 2014

100minds & Temple Street Children's Hospital

 

Spend Breakdown
Infographic

What an incredible achievement. To have raised €138,000 in just over 3 months is truly inspirational and a real testament to what can be achieved when a group of like minded young people set a goal. As someone who is familiar with overseeing fundraising events on a daily basis, I know how hard it can be to come up with a unique idea and rally support. But it didn’t seem to faze any of you and I was blown away at the ease in which you successfully project managed your own innovative events. From white collar boxing, charity balls and cake sales, to colouring competitions and the production of one minute twitter films, you all represented yourselves and Temple Street like true professionals. For this, I can’t thank you enough.

At present we are working hard to put the funds you raised to good use and I am thrilled to say we have been able to fund all aspects of the “Every Child Deserves A Childhood" category already. We have also been able to purchase specialist equipment for our Emergency Department, a new state of the art simulator doll for the Intensive Care Unit and many vital pieces of equipment for the Respiratory Department and Lab.

Supporting the family is a huge part of the work we do in Temple Street and with the money you raised we can now continue funding our much needed Bereavement Counselling Service and our Sibling Camps. This really is an amazing gift.

On behalf of every patient, parent, nurse, doctor and member of staff I would like to thank each and every one of you for giving up you precious time and taking part in 100minds. It’s thanks to your entrepreneurial flair, determination and support that we are able to continue to fund essential services through 2014 and purchase critical pieces of equipment. Please know the benefit of your endeavours will continue to ripple throughout our special hospital for many years to come.

Thank you all so much again from the bottom of my heart.

Denise Fitzgerald Temple Street Children's Hospital

Taking part in 100minds stands out as one of the best experiences from my college years. The people I met, the skills I developed and the targets we achieved all contributed to this. Myself and three friends raised over 6,000 euro for Temple Street Children's Hospital from our tag rugby event over the christmas break which we were extremely proud of. As a whole the experience taught me the importance of passion and teamwork to achieve a goal no matter how big or small. The level of help always available from 100minds mentors for both organising events and career advise was incredible. I would strongly recommend all third year students to apply for 100minds - not only are you in it to achieve an incredible target, you learn very valuable skills and meet many like minded individuals along the way.

Ellen Butler 100 Minds 2013 participant

100 minds was one the best initiatives and experiences I have ever took part in. It strenghtened my entrepreneurial and innovation skills. Arranging a talent show with a fellow 100minds member was a big task but it was a huge success and we exceeded our target. I am so proud to be part of this incredible organisation and give back to hospital who helped me a lot. It enabled me to make some great lifelong connections and friends and would recommend this to anyone who believes in social action and cares about making a difference in the lives of so many.

Alan Quinn Byrne 100 Minds 2013 participant

Taking part in 100minds was one of the best decisions I made this year. The experience provided me with an opportunity to put to work skills and knowledge I learned from college, develop my skillset and gain real life experience. What is even more important is that I was able to contribute to a cause which makes a real difference, Temple Street.

It has to be said that nothing worth doing is easy and that is certainly accurate when it came to 100minds. Thinking if an event, working out the when to do it, how to promote it and guarantee it's success is difficult. In my case I struggled to think of an idea which was feasible given the timeframe we had and without effecting my final year exams or study. Eventually I stopped trying to think of something extraordinary and settled what I could do well and which was without fuss. I organised two small events: a Tapas night in a local restaurant for family and friends and a Christmas dinner for close friends. Both events were small and simple and easy to set up, organise and control.

I would encourage everyone who can to take part in 100minds, it builds skills sought after by employers and allows you to really make a difference to your CV and a worthwhile family. At the beginning I was scared I wouldn't make the €1000 target but I surprised myself by exceeding it. You really don't know what you can do until get up and try.

Claire Diamond 100 Minds 2013 participant

100minds was by far the best thing I did in my first year in College and I would highly recommend getting involved this year. The greatest asset for me in taking part in 100minds was that it provided an excellent platform and support network to turn potential ideas into great events and to channel that energy to such an amazing cause like Temple Street. I had always been into organising things and had some good experience doing so but two crucial ideas, online ticket sales and the chosen date, which made the fight night so successful came from talking it over with the 100minds team.

So if you've no idea where to start or how to go about raising the €1,000 target, fear not! There are so many bright people involved to kickstart your campaign or give it a booster along the way. Become part of it.

John Lynch 100minds Top fundraiser 2013, raised €15,717
